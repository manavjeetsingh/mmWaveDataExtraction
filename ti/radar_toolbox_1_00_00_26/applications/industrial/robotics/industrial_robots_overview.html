Summary

Industrial robots are creating smart and innovative factories. They are used extensively in manufacturing to perform repetitive tasks such as assembly and welding, and typically operate inside cages or fences to protect operators against injury. Safety cages have been the traditional approach to safeguarding humans from industrial robots, however this practice has its drawbacks:

  1. Safety cages take up space and limit manufacturers ability to add more robots on the factory floor
  2. Stopping the robot every time a human enters the safety cage leads to increased cycle times (time for robot to complete one operation), thereby reducing manufacturing productivity

Customers are using TI mmWave based area scanners for Industrial robot safeguarding to improve manufacturing productivity without compromising operator safety.

TIs Offering

What can TIs IWR6843 / IWR6843AOP mmWave radar sensors offer for Industrial Robot Safeguarding?

  1. Define precise warning and keep out zones around the robot to create a virtual safety guard, eliminating the need for safety cages
  2. Enable accurate measurement of distance plus relative velocities of objects allowing robot to take more predictive action, such as stopping based on speed of approach
  3. Integrated tracking algorithms help improve productivity by avoiding stops due to false triggers if operator is moving away from the machine
  4. Immunity to environmental factors such as debris, sparks, smoke and lighting conditions on factory floors offers increased reliability over LiDAR and cameras in similar conditions
  5. IEC 61508 SIL 2 certification and supporting Functional Safety collateral help streamline system safety certification for faster time to market
